## Full listing description

### A character home with serious bones, ready for someone to finish the story

Tucked into a quiet residential soi just inland from Koh Lanta Old Town, this single-storey home is far more than its modest price suggests. The owners built it in 2016 with an unusual eye for design — a split-pitch butterfly roof, a vaulted living and dining room with tall transom windows, sliding wooden barn doors on black iron rails, and polished microcement bathrooms that wouldn't look out of place in a boutique guesthouse on Phuket's east coast.

Then life happened. The owners moved to mainland Krabi with a young child and never got the chance to finish what they started. The interior is largely move-in ready. The exterior wants paint and the garden needs a vision. That's where the opportunity sits.

### Why this is an investor's pitch

Koh Lanta tourism is climbing year on year, and Old Town has quietly become one of the island's most desirable corners — slower, more characterful, and increasingly popular with long-stay visitors, expats, and digital nomads who want to be close to the cafés, restaurants, and waterfront without paying west-coast beach prices. This house sits inside that catchment.

For roughly 1.95M THB plus a modest renovation budget — fresh exterior paint, some landscaping, a higher privacy fence on the east and west sides, and the addition of a back deck overlooking the greenery — a buyer ends up with a finished property in a strong rental position. Long-term tenants will pay well for something this design-forward at this distance from Old Town. High-season nightly rentals on Koh Lanta are a separate conversation entirely.

The owners are selling as-is, with no further work planned. The next owner gets to add the value.
